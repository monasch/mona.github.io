I'm a second year ELLIS PhD student at the Amsterdam Machine Learning Lab supervised by Eric Nalisnick and Dan Zhang. My PhD research focuses on out-of-distribution detection and generalization. Previously, I wrote my Master's thesis on continuous-time modeling under the supervision of Maja Rudolph at the Bosch Center for Artificial Intelligence. I'm also a machine learning consultant at the World Bank where I work with Sam Fraiberger on inferring causal relations from development economics literature.

I graduated with a joint Master's in statistics from the Technical University Berlin and Humboldt University Berlin and hold a French engineering degree in applied mathematics and economics from ENSAE.
